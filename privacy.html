Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Who we are

Kiddoe is a childcare management app created by Kiddoe Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales. Kiddoe was founded by Emma Woodhouse, a Norland-trained nanny. We are the data controller for the personal information you provide through the app.

If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at emma@kiddoe.app.

Our legal basis for processing your data

Kiddoe Ltd is subject to UK GDPR as a UK-registered company. We process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance. We process your data to provide you with the Kiddoe service you have signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests. We process limited technical data to keep the app running securely and reliably.
  • Consent. Where we ask for your consent (for example, for notifications), you can withdraw it at any time.

What information Kiddoe stores

Kiddoe stores only the information you actively choose to add. Nothing is gathered in the background without your knowledge.

Information you add to your family profile:

  • Your name and email address when you create an account
  • Your children's first names and dates of birth
  • Details you choose to share about your children including personality, interests, developmental milestones, dietary requirements and allergies
  • Photos you choose to upload including child profile photos, journal photos and family photos
  • Household information you add including medication details, incident logs, handover notes and schedules
  • Payment information, processed securely through Apple's in-app purchase system. Kiddoe never sees or stores your card details.

Information created within the app:

  • Activity suggestions and journal entries you save
  • Meal plans and recipe preferences you build
  • Schedule events and caregiver hours you log
  • Baby tracking entries you record

Basic technical information:

  • Device type and operating system version, used only to ensure the app works correctly on your device

How we use your information

Everything stored in Kiddoe is used for one purpose: to provide you with a better, more personalized childcare experience. Specifically we use it to:

  • Generate personalized activity suggestions and developmental insights for your children
  • Sync information between parents and caregivers within the same family account
  • Send notifications you have opted into, such as medication reminders and schedule alerts
  • Provide customer support when you need it

We do not use your data for advertising. We do not sell your data to third parties. Ever.

How your data is protected

All data is stored securely using Supabase, a trusted and widely used infrastructure provider. Your data is protected by:

  • Row Level Security. A technical control that means families can only ever see their own data. It is not technically possible for one family to access another family's information.
  • Encrypted connections. All data sent between your device and our servers is encrypted in transit.
  • Encrypted storage. All data stored in our database is encrypted at rest.
  • Private photo storage. Photos are never publicly accessible. They can only be viewed by authenticated members of your family account.

How we use AI technology

Kiddoe uses artificial intelligence to generate personalized activity suggestions and developmental insights. This technology is provided by Anthropic.

Anthropic operates a zero data retention policy for Kiddoe. This means the content of your requests, including your children's information, is not stored by Anthropic, is not used to train AI models, and is not retained after your request is processed.

Your children's data is never used to train any AI model. Meal ideas in Kiddoe come from a pre-built curated recipe library and do not use AI technology.

International data transfers

Kiddoe Ltd is a UK company. Some of our third-party providers, including Supabase and Anthropic, may process your data outside the UK. Where this happens, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in line with UK GDPR requirements, including standard contractual clauses where applicable.

Children's privacy (COPPA and UK GDPR)

Kiddoe is designed to be used by parents and professional caregivers. We take children's privacy extremely seriously. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) for our US users and with UK GDPR for all users.

Who uses Kiddoe. Children do not create accounts or interact with Kiddoe directly. Only authenticated adult parents and invited caregivers can access the app. By creating a family account and adding a child's profile, you confirm that you are the parent or legal guardian of that child and consent to Kiddoe storing their information as described in this policy.

What information we store about children. When you choose to add it, Kiddoe may store a child's first name, date of birth, photo, personality traits, interests, dietary requirements, allergies, developmental milestones and any additional notes you add to their profile. All of this information is provided voluntarily by the parent or caregiver.

How we use children's information. Information about your children is used solely to personalize their experience within the app. It is never used for advertising, never shared with third parties for commercial purposes and never used to train AI models.

Your rights as a parent. You can review, update or delete your child's information at any time within the app. To delete all information associated with your child, go to Settings or contact us at emma@kiddoe.app. We will respond within 30 days.

If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information without parental consent, please contact us immediately at emma@kiddoe.app and we will delete it promptly.

Who can see your information

Your information is shared only in these limited circumstances:

  • Within your family account. Parents and caregivers you have invited can access shared family data. You control who is invited and can remove access at any time.
  • Supabase. Stores your data securely on our behalf, bound by a strict data processing agreement.
  • Anthropic. Processes activity generation requests with zero data retention.
  • Apple. Processes payments securely. We never see your card details.
  • Amazon Associates. The Explore section contains affiliate links to Amazon products. Clicking them takes you to Amazon's website, which has its own privacy policy. We earn a small commission on qualifying purchases at no cost to you.
  • Legal requirements. We may disclose information if required to do so by law, including by UK regulatory authorities.

We never sell your data. We never share your data for advertising purposes.

Your rights under UK GDPR

As a UK company we are required to uphold your rights under UK GDPR. You have the right to:

  • Access your data. Request a copy of everything we hold about your family.
  • Correct your data. Update any information directly within the app at any time.
  • Delete your data. Delete your account and all associated data permanently in Settings, or by emailing emma@kiddoe.app. This cannot be undone.
  • Export your data. Request your data in a portable format.
  • Object to processing. Object to how we process your data in certain circumstances.
  • Withdraw consent. Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Lodge a complaint. You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe we have not handled your data correctly.

We will respond to any request within 30 days. Contact us at emma@kiddoe.app.

How long we keep your data

Your data is stored for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, all your family's data is permanently deleted within 30 days.

Amazon Associates disclosure

Kiddoe participates in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program. When you tap a product link in the Explore section, we may earn a small commission on qualifying purchases at no additional cost to you.

Changes to this policy

We will notify you of any significant changes to this policy by email or through the app. The date at the top shows when it was last updated.
